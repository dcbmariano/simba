What is SIMBA?


SIMBA, SImple Manager for Bacterial Assemblies, is a Web interface for managing various processes of assembly of bacterial genomes.

Simba was created to assist bioinformaticians to assemble bacterial genomes sequenced with next-generation platforms (NGS) quickly, easily and effectively.


Why use SIMBA?

SIMBA allows bioinformaticians do not worry so much about techniques with repetitive activities and focus on the most important: understand and analyze biological questions!

Mounts genomes using processes with a high degree of complexity that adopt heuristics to attempt to obtain results closer to the biological reality. Soon, dozens of software is required for completion of all assembly processes.

Thus, to realize a fitting is required, in addition to knowledge of the biological part, a large knowledge of operating system and the specific operation of each software. Which leads to a slow learning curve!

In addition, many repetitive processes could be reduced with the adoption of automation scripts, which controlled graphical interface, accelerate the process of data processing, assembly and curation.


How SIMBA works?

SIMBA runs on the Web! Can be run through any browser on any operating system.
SIMBA runs even cellular phones.

SIMBA is a wrapper, that integrates multiple tools into a single interface that can be accessed through a browser.

Genome assembly

Understanding what is genome assembly

Before explaining the operation of Simba is necessary to understand the problem of genome assembly.
Current DNA sequencing platforms (with the exception of PacBio) are able to read with only minor accuracy of DNA. Thus, prior to sequence the need arises to break the DNA into small pieces.
After sequencing, the need to order all the readings taken in the original position arises again, but there is no template to do this!
One solution is to break several different DNA molecules and cells belonging to the same organism at random points, and use the overlap between them to build the original genome again.

Source: (adapted) HUSEMANN, P. Bioinformatic Approaches for Genome Finishing, 2011.


Sort all readings without the help of a reference is called Mount new .
However, due to repetitive regions and the regions that tend to have low coverage sequencing, software Mounting new are not able to automatically reorganize the genome. So, give the name of contig sequence formed by the maximum possible overlaps between reads.
After a montage new , several contigs are formed, it is necessary now, sort them.
For ordering you can use a reference! A genome of an organism that is phylogenetically, one uses next and is ordered based on it.
Finally, a stage curated assembly, in which it tries to figure out what sequence is required between contig one another and, thus, to have the complete sequence.
